McSpicy Burger — Exact Copycat Recipe

★★★★★ 4.9 (312 ratings)

That unmistakable crunch, the perfectly seasoned patty, the signature sauce — all recreated with pantry staples. The one you'll make every week.

Ingredients
  • 500g chicken breast, pounded
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 cup plain flour
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 brioche buns
  • 4 tbsp spicy mayo sauce

Instructions
  1. 01

    Pound chicken breast to an even 1cm thickness. Combine buttermilk, cayenne, garlic powder and 1 tsp salt in a bowl. Submerge chicken and marinate at least 1 hour — overnight is best for maximum flavour penetration.

  2. 02

    Mix flour, cornstarch, paprika, salt and pepper in a shallow dish. Remove chicken from buttermilk, let excess drip off, then dredge firmly in the flour mix. Press it in — those craggy bits are what creates the crunch.

  3. 03

    Heat oil to 175°C (350°F) in a heavy-bottomed pan. Fry chicken 4–5 minutes per side until deep golden. Internal temperature must reach 74°C. Drain on a wire rack — never paper towels, which steam and kill the crust.

  4. 04

    Toast buns cut-side down in a dry pan for 60 seconds. Spread spicy sauce on both halves. Stack chicken, pickles and shredded lettuce. Serve immediately — this does not hold.

Pro Tips
  • Double-dredge for extra crunch: dip floured chicken back into buttermilk then flour again.
  • Oil temperature is everything. Use a thermometer — too cool means greasy, too hot means raw inside.
  • Keep finished pieces hot in a 100°C oven on a wire rack while you fry remaining batches.
Variations
01
Air Fryer Version
Spray well with oil. Cook at 200°C for 12–15 min, flipping halfway. The crust won't be identical but it's an excellent lighter option.
02
Extra Spicy
Double the cayenne and add 1 tsp of gochugaru to both the marinade and the flour dredge. Finish with chilli oil in the sauce.
03
Buttermilk-Free
Mix 1 cup whole milk + 1 tbsp white vinegar, let sit 5 minutes. Works identically as a buttermilk substitute.
FAQ
Can I use an air fryer instead of deep frying?+
Yes — spray well with oil and cook at 200°C for 12–15 min, flipping halfway. Crust won't be identical but it's a great lighter option.
What oil is best for frying?+
Neutral high smoke-point oils only: vegetable, canola, or peanut oil. Avoid olive oil — its smoke point is too low.
Can I make these ahead of time?+
Marinated chicken keeps refrigerated up to 24 hours. Fried chicken is best fresh — reheat in oven at 180°C for 10 min to restore crispness.
